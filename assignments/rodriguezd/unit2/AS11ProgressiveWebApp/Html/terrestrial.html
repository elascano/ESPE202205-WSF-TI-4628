The lion

The King of the Jungle is one of th e largest felines that exist and is undoubtedly the quintessential wild animal. It is a strong and powerful carnivorous animal, weighing about two hundred kilos on average and whose social structure is herds. The males are characterized by having a long mane whose functions are to appear larger and protect their throat from possible attacks.

The wolf

The second of the most well-known wild animals in the world is the wolf. It is an animal from which the rest of the dog breeds come, which were later domesticated. The wolf is a very active animal, with great momentum and many skills. They weigh approximately 50 kg and can live up to 15 or 16 years. They are social animals that also live in herds and communicate through the well-known howls, but also by wagging their tails and ears.

Elephant

Elephants are one of the largest and heaviest animals in the world, weighing up to 10,000 kilos. Even the young, when they are born, already weigh more than 100 kilos. They are especially recognized because they have a spectacular elongated trunk or nose that serves them for many different uses, and they also have spectacular ivory tusks. They also have striking ears that help them regulate their temperature.

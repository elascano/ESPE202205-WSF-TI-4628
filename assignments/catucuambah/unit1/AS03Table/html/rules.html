Security Decalogue

		1. You believe that people tend to do things well. Do not underestimate the power of human stupidity.		
		2. When you think that nobody would think of doing something like this, there is always someone thinking that it is a good idea.
		3. In many companies there is never time to do things well, but there is time to do them twice.
		4. To break into a system most of the time you only need the most powerful tool ever created: Notepad
		5. If you are one of those who thinks that you have nothing that an attacker is interested in, then you already have a Trojan on your computer.
		6. When you upload something to the Internet, sooner or later it will be public. face it
		7. Tell me what your website is and I will tell you what your bug is.
		8. Breaking into a system requires knowledge of a security flaw, defending it requires shutting down all of them.
		9. You will pay all the money in the world to secure your systems once you have been hacked and publicly exposed.
		10. If you don't audit the security of your systems, someone else will do it for you and for free.
		11. Things work by chance. And the worst thing is that almost all of us know it.
		12. The bigger the company, the more scandalous the security flaws.
		13. You will recognize a hacker conference because no one uses a computer or smartphone in it for security.
		14. The sum of the things you know are wrong and the things you don't know gives Pwned.
		15. And now you Tweet it.

Civil Engineering



Civil engineering is the discipline in charge of carrying out all kinds of infrastructures that society needs for its development, always trying to carry them out in the most economical and sustainable way possible. To do this, they must be able to understand a multitude of factors (technical, economic and social) that affect infrastructures in one way or another and act accordingly.

What a Civil Engineer do?
Roads and highways, bridges and footbridges, dams and reservoirs, drinking water supply networks and sanitation networks (sewerage), ports, urban developments and many other infrastructures are the responsibility of the civil engineer. But not everything is building. Economic aspects and aspects of management and exploitation of these infrastructures are also part of the main axis of many tasks carried out by civil engineers around the world.

What specialties exist?
Civil engineering is very broad and it would be impossible to cover it in its entirety. In the Degree in Civil and Territorial Engineering that I study, and once you have passed the first three years, you can choose one of these specialties:

• Civil Constructions: you will specialize in the calculation of structures, construction projects, buildings, bridges, tunnels, foundations, etc. It is, without a doubt, the most complicated specialty.
• Transport and Urban Services: you will specialize in the design of roads, railways, traffic management, port operations, etc.
• Hydrology: it will specialize in the design and construction of dams, other hydraulic infrastructures (canals), energy and power plants, etc.

What skills should a civil engineer have?
I want to take away from you the idea that by studying civil engineering you are going to eat the world like that just because. You will have the bricks, but you will have to work very hard to build the house. The competition is brutal. Other aspects such as mastery of a second language (especially English), communication skills (public speaking), leadership and critical thinking are essential. The domain of agile methodologies and BIM are also increasingly important.

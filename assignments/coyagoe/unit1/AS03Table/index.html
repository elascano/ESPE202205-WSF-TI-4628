Student Obligations





- Abide by the rules and regulations contained in the ESPE University Legislation.
- Do not falsify official documents, such as certificates, birth certificates, etc.
- Avoid events that weaken and harm the University.
- Respect your peers in their way of thinking and being. Do not physically or verbally attack them.
- Take care of and respect the facilities of your campus and of the ESPE.
- Do not drink intoxicating or narcotic beverages inside and outside the university campus.
- Do not attempt against the health of students by offering or selling them drinks, narcotics, psychotropics or inhalants.
- Do not create disorders within any campus or other instance of the ESPE.
- Respect teachers, classmates and any member of the community.
- Do not cheat on tests or receive inappropriate help.
- Do not carry weapons.

Rights






- Be part of the university community when the ESPE declares you admitted and complies with the respective procedures for your registration.
- Right to gender equality.
- To be treated with respect regardless of your sexual preference and way of thinking.
- Enroll up to twice in each subject, within the four years established in the general enrollment regulations.
- Receive all your classes or at least 85% of the established ones and with no more than ten minutes late by the teacher.
- Receive in print the record of your subjects to study.
- Obtain a certificate of studies upon completion of high school.
- Elect student representatives before the University Council, Technical Council and Internal Council of the respective campuses.
- Have access to the ESPE Library System.
- Have the credential issued by the Institution that accredits you as a university student.
- Enjoy the sports and recreational facilities of the ESPE.

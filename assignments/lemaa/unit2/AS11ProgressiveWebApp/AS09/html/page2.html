Star Wars, also known in Spanish as La Guerra de las galaxias, is a franchise composed primarily of a series of films conceived by the American filmmaker George Lucas in the 1970s, and produced and distributed by The Walt Disney Company from 2012.

Its plot describes the experiences of a group of characters who live in a fictional galaxy and interact with elements such as "the Force", a metaphysical and omnipresent energy field that has a "light side" driven by wisdom, nobility and justice. and used by the Jedi, and a "dark side" used by the Sith and brought about by anger, fear, and hatred. The first film in the series, Star Wars: Episode IV - A New Hope (1977). Star Wars: Episode V - The Empire Strikes Back (1980) and Star Wars: Episode VI - Return of the Jedi (1983), which completed the called the "original trilogy". Almost two decades later, Star Wars: Episode I - The Phantom Menace (1999) was released, followed by Star Wars: Episode II - Attack of the Clones (2002) and Star Wars: Episode III - Revenge of the Sith ( 2005). At the end of 2012, Disney acquired Lucasfilm, producer of the previous six films, and announced the completion of a third film trilogy consisting of Star Wars: Episode VII - The Force Awakens (2015), Star Wars: Episode VIII - The Last Jedi (2017) and Star Wars: Episode IX - The Rise of Skywalker (2019), franchise extensions such as Rogue One (2016) and Han Solo (2018).

In addition to the cinematographic field, the franchise includes a wide variety of products such as novels, television series, video games, comics, theme park attractions, role-playing, war or miniature games and toys, which make up an important part of what is known as Star Wars "expanded universe".

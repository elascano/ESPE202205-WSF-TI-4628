SCOOBY DOO

Scooby-Doo is a franchise based around a popular American animated television series produced by Hanna-Barbera Productions in multiple versions from its premiere on CBS in 1969 to the present.

The show's format and cast have changed significantly over the years. The most well-known versions include a talking Great Dane named Scooby-Doo and four teenagers named Fred Jones, Daphne Blake, Vilma Dinkley and Shaggy Rogers, who travel the world in a van called "La MÃ¡quina Del Misterio". At the end of each episode, the supernatural forces have a rational explanation, usually a criminal who scares people so he can commit his crimes.

Scooby-Doo was first broadcast in the period between 1969 and 1976. A new series titled What's New Scooby-Doo? was broadcast on the WB Television Network between 2002 and 2005, until another series, Shaggy and Scooby -Doo detectives, was launched by CW network since 2006.

Scooby-Doo has maintained a significant fan base over the years, growing especially in the 1990s due to the popularity of the show, becoming a cult classic among children of all ages, young and nostalgic adults who grew up with the original series. During its more than 4 decades of existence, Scooby-Doo has received various awards, including two nominations for the Emmy Awards.

What is Literature?
Literature is an artistic manifestation based on the use of words and language, both written and oral. It is also the name of an academic subject and the name of the theory that studies literary works. The word literature derives from the Latin literature. With this term is also known the set of literary productions, for example, of a country, a language or a time. By extension, it is also used to refer to the set of works that deal with a specific topic.

Among the most outstanding authors for creating some of the most important works of literature, we can mention:

• William Shakespeare or Edgar Allan Poe (literature in English).
• Miguel de Cervantes or Gabriel García Márquez (literature in Spanish).
• Johann Wolfgang von Goethe or Frank Kafka (literature in German).
• Victor Hugo or Albert Camus (literature in French).
• Fyodor Dostoyevsky or Leo Tolstoy (Russian literature).
